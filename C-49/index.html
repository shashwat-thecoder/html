Making a Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  • Thanks to thick pads of skin on their chest and knees, camels can comfortably sit in very hot sand.



Making a Chamaleon

  • Their feet work like salad tongs
  • Almost half of all known speceies live in Madagascar
  • Chamaleons vary wildly in terms of size
  • Changle colour to indicate body temperature or to communicate
  • Unlike many lizards, they can't regrow their tails



Making a Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus
  • Most have red-orange eyes
  • Females may be attracted to the sound of motors
  • Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs



Making a Panda

  • A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black
  • Pandas have so many fans because they look cute
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day



Making a Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
